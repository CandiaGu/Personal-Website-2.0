miniRx | Rothberg Catalyzer Impact-a-Thon

Winter 2018

For this week-long event, we were to create an economical and impactful solution to a pressing social issue. This year's prompt was to seek solutions to the problem of medical relief during disasters. Storms, earthquakes, wildfires, floods tornados and other weather-related disasters put people, communities, and infrastructure at risk, requiring rapid response in a variety of ways. To address this issue, my team and I developed a system that uses facial recognition to provide the correct prescriptions to those who need it.

For the software portion, my team used opencv for facial recognition and firebase to store the face images, as well as the prescription information. On the hardware side, I used an arduino to control the motors used to dispense the medicine. We designed the mechanism to resemble that of a vending machine where the coils were attached to stepper motor which then rotated a corresponding number of times to push out the pills.

Our prints and logo were designed by Chris Reyes, who handled the design portion of our project.
