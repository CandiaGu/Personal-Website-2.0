HoloPicto | Build18

Winter 2018

For this week-long event hosted by the Electrical and Computer Engineering Dept at CMU, my team and I designed and constructed an editable hologram controlled using hand gestures.

To do this, we used a leapmotion sensor to detect hand gestures and a TV reflected onto an acrylic pyramid to create the illusion that the objects are in 3-dimensional space. We used java in processing to render the 3-D graphics and manipulate the shapes drawn.

Deep Learning | Xometry Internship

Summer 2017

During my internship at Xometry, I worked on several projects with different teams in the company. The most notable one I worked on a deep learning project in which I had to cluster various CAD parts based on thumbnail images and shape distributions. I researched into machine learning and deep learning (see here) and compiled my findings of the results of clustering into a jupyter notebook.

Some other tools I used during this process were scikit-learn for its deep learning library, MongoDB to retrieve the shape distribution data and other data on the parts’ geometry, and AWS for its graphics processing capabilities.

Electric Skateboard with Tilt Detection | Build18

Summer 2017

For this week-long event hosted by the Electrical and Computer Engineering Dept at CMU, my team and I designed an electric skateboard that would speed up and slow down depending on the user’s tilt.

To do this, we used an arduino to control the speed of the motor depending on the force on the pressure sensors. We also built the circuit that would translate the signal from the arduino to the motors.
